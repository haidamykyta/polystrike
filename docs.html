FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

Everything you need to know before you start.

What is PolyStrike?

PolyStrike is a suite of analytical tools for managing risk on prediction markets. It helps you model payoff scenarios, backtest positions, and find optimal hedges. No trading, no custody — just math and visualization.

How does hedging work?

You buy a NO position on Polymarket and pair it with a vanilla option on Bybit. The combined P&L reduces your maximum loss. If BTC moves against your PM position, the Bybit option offsets part of the damage.

What's Black-Scholes?

Black-Scholes is a mathematical model for pricing options. PolyStrike adapts it for Polymarket binary options — it estimates what a PM position "should" cost given time to expiry, volatility, and the underlying asset price.

Is it free?

Yes. All tools are completely free. No signup, no credit card, no wallet connection required. Open the app and start analyzing positions.

What exchanges are supported?

Polymarket for prediction market positions (YES/NO binary options) and Bybit for BTC vanilla options (calls and puts). The tools pull live data from both platforms.

Can I lose money using these tools?

The tools help you analyze and understand risk — they don't eliminate it. You still execute trades manually on Polymarket and Bybit. This is NOT a money button. Use the tools to model scenarios before you commit capital.

What's a "construction"?

A construction is a multi-leg position that combines PM binary options with Bybit vanilla options. By layering different instruments, you create a defined-risk profile — you know your max loss and profit zones before entering.

How do time curves work?

The P&L chart shows 4 lines: Now, +7 days, +14 days, and At Expiry. As time passes, options lose value (theta decay). The curves show how your position evolves — time decay is your friend when you're selling premium.

What's the "flat top"?

The flat top is the price range on the P&L chart where your maximum loss is minimal (around -2%). A wider flat top means a better hedge — BTC can move within that range without hurting your position.

Glossary

Key terms, explained simply.

No jargon walls. One-line definitions for everything in the app.

Binary Option

A contract that pays out a fixed amount (e.g. $1) if a condition is met, or $0 if not. Polymarket positions are binary options.

Strike Price

The price level at which an option becomes profitable. For BTC options: the target BTC price. For PM: the YES/NO threshold.

IV (Implied Volatility)

How much the market expects the price to move. Higher IV means options cost more because bigger swings are expected.

Theta (Time Decay)

Options lose value as they approach expiry. Theta measures how much value is lost per day. Sellers benefit, buyers pay for it.

Delta

How much an option's price moves when the underlying asset moves $1. Delta 0.5 means the option moves 50 cents per $1 of BTC movement.

Construction

A multi-leg position combining PM binary options with Bybit vanilla options to create a specific risk/reward profile.

Payoff Diagram

A chart showing your profit or loss at every possible price of the underlying asset. The main visualization in PolyStrike tools.

Spread

A position with two options at different strikes. Limits both your max profit and max loss. Used in Auto-Hedger for defined-risk setups.

Straddle

Buying both a call and a put at the same strike. Profits when BTC makes a big move in either direction.

Moon Bag

A small position left open after taking profit, designed to capture outsized returns if the market moves dramatically in your favor.

Flat Top Zone

The price range on a P&L chart where your loss stays minimal (around -2%). A wider flat top means a more effective hedge.
