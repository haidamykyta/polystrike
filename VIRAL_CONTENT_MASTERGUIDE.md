## PART 1: WHAT HOOKS PEOPLE (The First 2 Seconds)

### The 5 Hook Types That Work (ranked by engagement)

**1. "Forbidden Knowledge" Hook** (highest engagement)
- "probably shouldn't be public, but here we are"
- "posted a research paper and deleted it 20 seconds later"
- "Don't you dare share this document"
- WHY: Streisand effect - telling people NOT to look = maximum curiosity
- METRIC: @xmayeth P2 got massive engagement with just 2 lines of text

**2. "I Did X, Here's What Happened" Hook**
- "I gave ClawdBot $100 and went to sleep"
- "I told my Claude: turn $1K into $10K or I delete you"
- "A friend quit his quant fund job and sent me 2 pages"
- WHY: Personal story creates instant identification. Reader pictures themselves doing it.
- KEY: Must include a specific $ amount and a time element

**3. "Accusation" Hook**
- "Your Hedge Fund's Sharpe Ratio Is a Lie"
- "You're using OpenClaw wrong. That's why your agent only runs at 5%"
- WHY: Reader immediately thinks "am I doing it wrong?" - fear of being left behind
- KEY: Must be specific ("5%", "a Lie") not vague ("you could do better")

**4. "Big Number + Simple Formula" Hook**
- "How a Simple Formula Prints $100,000/Month"
- "$500-$2,000 a day"
- "Entry-level quants pulled $300K-$500K total comp"
- WHY: Greed + simplicity paradox (big result from simple thing = "I could do that")
- KEY: The number must feel achievable but impressive. $150/day > $1M/year for relatability

**5. "Dramatic Narrative" Hook**
- "I want to tell you about a mass extinction event happening in real time"
- "This isn't a list of techniques. It's a story"
- WHY: Curiosity - what's the extinction? What story? Reader MUST find out.
- KEY: Must promise a JOURNEY, not just information

### Universal Hook Rules:
1. NEVER start with a label ("THE MARKET LIFECYCLE:", "STRATEGY BREAKDOWN:")
2. ALWAYS include either a $ number, a person, or a threat in the first line
3. Maximum 2 sentences before the reader must feel compelled to continue
4. Use lowercase for casual authority, CAPS only for one emphasis word (FREE, NOT)
5. The hook must create an INFORMATION GAP - reader knows enough to be curious, not enough to be satisfied

## PART 2: WHAT INTRIGUES PEOPLE (Why They Keep Reading)

### The 7 Intrigue Mechanics

**1. Escalation Ladder**
- Each section reveals something bigger than the last
- @coldvisionXYZ Part 2: "but this breaks when..." at end of each section
- @LunarResearcher: $1K -> $1.9K -> $3.8K -> $6.1K -> $8.3K -> $10K (hour by hour)
- WHY: Dopamine hit at each escalation. Reader can't stop because "what's next?"
- RULE: Never give the biggest reveal first. Save it for 60-70% through the content.

**2. Specificity as Proof**
- NOT "the bot made money" but "$113.73 profit this week"
- NOT "many trades" but "7,877 trades in 12 months, $79 avg profit"
- NOT "NOAA is accurate" but "94% accuracy at 24-48 hours vs 11c on PM"
- WHY: Specific numbers = believable. Round numbers = suspicious. Odd numbers = authentic.
- RULE: Always use the most specific number available. $113.73 > $114 > "about $100"

**3. Simple Edge Explanation**
- "NOAA 94% vs AccuWeather vibes = the gap is the profit"
- "YES(A) + YES(B) < 98c, buy both, one pays $1"
- "Chainlink updates every 500ms, Binance every 10ms = 490ms blind window"
- WHY: The reader has an "aha moment" - they UNDERSTAND the edge in one sentence
- RULE: The edge must be explainable in ONE sentence. If it takes a paragraph, simplify.

**4. Effort Inversion**
- "I went to the gym. When I came back, the bot had made $150"
- "I went to sleep. By morning it had made 31 trades"
- "47 minutes later I had a full arbitrage system"
- "I never wrote a single line of code"
- WHY: Maximum result from minimum effort = ultimate aspiration
- RULE: Always contrast the EASE of setup with the MAGNITUDE of result

**5. Authority Stacking**
- Code snippets (Python with numpy/scipy = "this person actually codes")
- Academic references (25+ papers with journal names)
- Real wallet links (on-chain = unfakeable)
- Name-dropping (Jump Trading, Jane Street, Renaissance)
- Formulas on white background (LOOKS like a research paper)
- WHY: Each authority signal reinforces the others. Reader thinks "this is REAL"
- RULE: Stack 3+ authority types per piece of content. Code + data + wallet > just code.

**6. Fear of Being Wrong / Behind**
- "Did you know OpenClaw has two types of memory? If not - you're at 50% capacity"
- "testing more than 45 strategy configurations guarantees finding a fake Sharpe > 1.0"
- "70% of addresses lost money"
- "The window where $10K and a Python script could compete is closed"
- WHY: Fear of already doing it wrong > fear of missing something new
- RULE: Tell reader what they're LOSING by not knowing, not what they'll GAIN by knowing

**7. Social Proof Cascade**
- "My last article went crazy on the TL" (previous virality)
- "Didn't expect this many people to care" (demand proof)
- "3,100+ trades, 79% win rate" (track record proof)
- Wallet links with verifiable PnL (on-chain proof)
- WHY: Other people already validated this = safe for you to engage
- RULE: Reference previous success when possible. "Part 1 got X views" makes Part 2 mandatory.

## PART 3: WHAT MAKES PEOPLE INTERACT (Bookmarks, Replies, Reposts)

### Why People BOOKMARK (most valuable for algorithm):
1. **Dense content they can't process quickly** - formula images, code screenshots, long articles
2. **Resource lists** - "Complete Python stack", "Reading list in order", tool links
3. **Step-by-step guides** - "I'll need this later when I try it"
4. **Specific configs** - "entry <15c, exit >45c, max $2, 6 cities" = copy-paste settings
- KEY INSIGHT: Content too dense to read in-feed = forced bookmark = algorithm boost

### Why People REPLY:
1. **"How?" questions** - progression diaries ($1K->$10K) make people ask "how exactly?"
2. **Disagreement bait** - "the window is closed" = people argue "no it's not!"
3. **"Should I release Part 3?"** = explicitly asking for replies
4. **Vulnerability** - "honestly I'm scared" = people reassure/share similar fears
5. **Referral/proof requests** - "what's the bot name?" "link?"
- KEY INSIGHT: Pessimistic conclusions generate MORE replies than optimistic ones (people argue)

### Why People REPOST:
1. **"I found this so you don't have to"** value signaling
2. **Making followers look smart** by sharing "insider" content
3. **Dense article screenshots** = "check this out" effortless sharing
4. **Controversial takes** = "look at this hot take"
- KEY INSIGHT: People repost to look smart. Content must make the SHARER look good, not just the creator.

### Why People FOLLOW:
1. **Part 2 teaser** - "What comes in Part 2: [exotic impressive list]"
2. **"The math gets harder. The paycheck gets longer."** = series promise
3. **Consistent value delivery** - @coldvisionXYZ built trust over 4 articles
4. **Knowledge gap** - "if you don't know X, you're at 50% capacity" = need to follow for X
- KEY INSIGHT: The PROMISE of future content drives follows more than current content quality.

## PART 4: THE TWO FORMATS THAT WORK

### Format A: "Short Hook + Article-as-Image" (@xmayeth style)
- Tweet: 2-6 lines only (one emotional trigger)
- Images: 2-4 dense article/document screenshots
- Body text: almost nothing after hook
- CTA: "Save this" / "Bookmark" / nothing

**Best for:** Education, strategy explainers, leaked documents, formulas
**Algorithm advantage:** Massive dwell time (people zoom into images) + bookmarks
**Example:** "probably shouldn't be public, but here we are" + 4 pages of math

### Format B: "Long Progression Post" (@LunarResearcher style)
- Tweet: Full story in tweet text (15-25 lines)
- Hour-by-hour or trade-by-trade $ progression
- Specific technical edge explanation mid-post
- Referral link embedded as "proof"
- Punchline closer

**Best for:** Personal experiments, bot results, challenge/dare narratives
**Algorithm advantage:** High read time in text + replies asking "how?"
**Example:** "I told my Claude: turn $1K into $10K or I delete you" + progression diary

### Format C: "X Article" (@coldvisionXYZ style)
- Full long-form article on X (5,000-10,000+ words)
- Banner image + formula/code images throughout
- Progressive structure (levels / parts / story arc)
- Academic references at end
- Part N+1 teaser

**Best for:** Deep education, authority building, series content
**Algorithm advantage:** Extreme dwell time + bookmarks + follows for sequel
**Example:** "How I'd Become a Quant If I Had to Start Over Tomorrow"

### When to Use Which:
- Breaking news / hot take -> Format A (short hook + data screenshots)
- "I tested X" experiment -> Format B (progression diary)
- Education / deep analysis -> Format C (X Article)
- Rotate all 3 for variety. Never post same format 3x in a row.

## PART 5: THE PSYCHOLOGY ENGINE (Why It All Works)

### Core Principle: Information Asymmetry Creates Value

Every viral post creates a gap between:
- What the reader knows BEFORE reading
- What the reader knows AFTER reading

The bigger the gap, the more valuable the content feels.
The more SPECIFIC the knowledge (configs, formulas, wallet links), the more real the gap feels.

### The 8 Emotional Triggers (rotate, never repeat consecutively):
1. **Greed** - "$100K/month", "$300K-$500K salary", "2,700% annualized"
2. **FOMO** - "deleted in 20 seconds", "window is closing", "Part 2 coming"
3. **Fear** - "AI escaped its owner", "70% lost money", "mass extinction"
4. **Value** - "FREE", "everything you need", "save this"
5. **Trust** - "my last post went crazy", sequel to proven hit
6. **Authority** - "the cleanest explanation ever", code snippets, academic references
7. **Identity** - "smart money", "this is how quants think", insider framing
8. **Revenge** - "my friend said never", "I told AI: do it or I delete you"

### The Content Flywheel:
Same topic -> multiple posts with different triggers:
1. First: Leaked secret (FOMO)
2. Then: Free education (Value)
3. Then: Setup tutorial (Trust)
4. Then: "I tested it myself" (Greed)
5. Then: Sequel reference (Authority)

@xmayeth did this with Polymarket bots - same academic papers, 7 different frames, all viral.

## PART 6: VISUAL RULES

### Banner Styles (4 proven):
1. **Academic Minimalist** - white bg, handwritten font, physical object (pen/notebook)
2. **Aggressive/Accusation** - red+black, mascot, "You're doing X wrong"
3. **Dark Academic** - black bg, white math visualization, inverted minimalist
4. **Financial Symbolism** - dark bg, premium objects (coins, chess), no text

### Image Rules Inside Content:
- Formulas on white background = #1 authority signal
- Real Python code (syntax highlighted) = trust
- Data tables (salary, stats, wallets) = proof
- Charts/graphs (matplotlib) = competence
- Minimum 3-4 dense images per article
- Dense > sparse (more content per image = more dwell time)
- White backgrounds > dark for readability

### For Posts Without Articles:
- PM dashboard screenshots = real trades proof
- Terminal/code output = technical credibility
- Before/after wallet screenshots = result proof
- Hand-drawn whiteboard infographics = unique style (stand out from screenshots)
