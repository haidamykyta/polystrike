6 Proven Strategies

Options strategies adapted for prediction markets.

From 1.5 years of live trading on Polymarket. Traditional options playbooks rewritten for binary outcomes, bracket markets, and time-bound resolution.

01 — The Straddle

Buy BOTH YES and NO on the same market.

The classic volatility play. You don't need to know direction — you need the market to move. Buy both sides before a catalyst, profit when one side surges more than the other costs.

When to Use

  • Market near 50/50 (YES at 45c-55c)
  • Known catalyst approaching (earnings, vote, ruling)
  • High volatility expected but direction uncertain
  • You want exposure to movement, not outcome
Max Loss (hold to resolution) ~4-5%
Max Loss (early exit) ~2-5%
Profit Potential Unlimited on breakout
Key Insight

Pure 50/50 straddles break even by definition. The edge comes from pre-catalyst entry — buying before the market prices in the event, then selling the winning side during the volatility spike.

02 — The Bracket Spread (Bull/Bear)

Use related bracket markets as synthetic strike prices.

Polymarket bracket markets (e.g., "BTC $80K-$85K", "BTC $85K-$90K") behave like strike prices in traditional options. Buy one bracket YES, sell the adjacent bracket YES to create a defined-risk directional bet.

When to Use

  • Multiple related brackets exist for the same underlying
  • You have a directional view (bullish or bearish)
  • Want defined risk instead of binary all-or-nothing
  • Adjacent brackets are mispriced relative to each other
Risk Profile Defined (spread width)
Max Loss Spread debit
Max Profit $1 - spread debit
Key Insight

Spreads shine when you're CLOSE but not exact. If BTC ends at $84,900 instead of $85,100, a naked $85K+ bet loses 100%. A spread loses only the debit. Brackets let you be approximately right and still profit.

03 — The Iron Condor (Range Play)

Bet on the MIDDLE range outcomes. Skip the extremes.

When a market has 4+ brackets, the extremes are usually overpriced by retail excitement. Buy the middle 2-3 brackets (YES), skip the tails. You profit if the outcome lands anywhere in your range.

When to Use

  • Market has 4 or more outcome brackets
  • Extreme brackets are overpriced by hype/fear
  • You expect outcome in the middle range
  • Sum of middle brackets is less than fair value
Win Zone ~75% of market (middle brackets)
Max Loss Total premium paid
Win Probability High (wide range)
Key Insight

A PM condor is a concentrated bet on 2-3 outcomes, not a true 4-leg options condor. The edge comes from retail overpricing the tails. "BTC hits $120K this month" at 8c is expensive when your model says 2%. Sell those tails, own the middle.

04 — The Butterfly (Precision Bet)

Concentrate on ONE bracket. Hedge with adjacent wings.

High conviction on a specific outcome? Load up on that bracket, then buy small positions on adjacent brackets as insurance. If you hit the target, massive payout. If you miss slightly, the wings soften the blow.

When to Use

  • HIGH conviction on a specific bracket outcome
  • Target bracket is cheap (15c-30c range)
  • Adjacent brackets provide reasonable insurance
  • Risk/reward of naked bet feels too binary
Upside (target hit) +135% or more
Downside (with wings) -50% (vs -100% naked)
Best Entry Price 15c - 30c bracket
Key Insight

The butterfly cuts your max loss in half while keeping most of the upside. A 25c bracket naked risks -100% for +300%. With wings, you risk -50% for +135%. The risk-adjusted return is dramatically better. Be precise, but be protected.

05 — The Asymmetric Straddle POLYSTRIKE ORIGINAL

Weight your straddle toward the side you think MORE likely.

A pure straddle is 50/50. But what if you're 65% sure it's YES? Weight your position: 65% on YES, 35% on NO. You keep volatility protection, but juice the profitable side. Same total risk, higher expected value.

When to Use

  • You have a directional view but aren't certain
  • Want straddle protection with a directional tilt
  • Market is near a catalyst but you lean one way
  • Want to size up while maintaining the same risk envelope
Allocation Example 65% YES / 35% NO
If Correct Side Wins Higher profit than pure straddle
If Wrong Side Wins Still profitable (NO leg pays)
Key Insight

The secret: you can size up with the same risk as a pure directional bet. A $100 naked YES bet risks $100. A $100 asymmetric straddle (65/35) risks less on the wrong side and profits more on the right side. Same capital, better risk-adjusted return.

06 — The Calendar Play (Time-Based)

Trade the same question across different timeframes.

Polymarket often has the same question for different dates: "BTC hits $100K in March" and "BTC hits $100K in Q1". The shorter-dated market resolves first, giving you a free information event that reprices the longer-dated position.

When to Use

  • Same underlying question exists for different time periods
  • Shorter market resolves before the longer one
  • Mispricing between the two timeframes
  • Want to use one resolution as a free signal for the other
Structure Long short-term + Long/Short long-term
Edge Source Free information event
Time Horizon Days to weeks
Key Insight

Shorter resolution is a free information event. When the March market resolves NO, the Q1 market reprices instantly. If you're positioned correctly in both, the shorter market's resolution pays for your longer market's position adjustment. Time is the edge.
